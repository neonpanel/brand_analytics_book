## Strategic Recommendations

### The Big Picture

The data tells a clear story: **your products convert well once shoppers click, but not enough shoppers are clicking.** Top ASINs show purchase rates of 20-25% — that's strong. But every single ASIN in the catalog has a red flag for low click-through rate. This means the problem is not your product or your pricing at checkout — it's how your listings appear in search results. Fix the click, and the existing conversion engine does the rest.

Meanwhile, keyword momentum is flat. No terms are accelerating, none are collapsing. You're in a holding pattern. The recommendations below are designed to break out of it.

---

### Priority 1: Fix the Click-Through Rate Crisis (Week 1-2)

**Why this is #1:** Every ASIN has a red weakness for low CTR. This is a portfolio-wide problem that caps your entire revenue ceiling. Your top ASIN (B0DSTD2XWC) gets 703,753 impressions but only 1.59% CTR. At the category benchmark of ~3%, that's **~10,000 clicks left on the table every week** — and at your 13.35% purchase rate, that's roughly **700 additional orders/week from one ASIN alone.**

**What to do:**

1. **A/B test main images on your top 3 ASINs immediately**
   - B0DSTD2XWC (703K impressions, 1.59% CTR)
   - B0B61478T9 (286K impressions, 2.53% CTR)
   - B0CPJYWXV7 (39K impressions, 2.44% CTR)
   - Use Amazon's "Manage Your Experiments" to run main image tests
   - Study competitors ranking on your top keywords — what do their main images show that yours don't? (lifestyle vs. product-only, angle, packaging visibility, infographic overlays)

2. **Audit titles for your top 5 ASINs**
   - Ensure the primary keyword appears in the first 80 characters
   - Check that key buying factors (size, material, use case) are visible in the truncated mobile title
   - Compare side-by-side with the top 3 competitors on "coccyx cushion" and "seat cushion"

3. **Review pricing position**
   - Check if your listings appear significantly more expensive than competitors in search results
   - Even if your product is premium, consider a coupon badge — the orange tag increases CTR by 10-20% in most categories

4. **Check review counts and ratings**
   - If any top ASIN is below 4.0 stars or under 100 reviews, this is a CTR killer
   - Consider enrolling in Amazon Vine for newer ASINs or increasing review request follow-ups

**Expected impact:** Moving B0DSTD2XWC from 1.59% to 2.5% CTR = ~6,400 additional clicks/week = ~850 additional orders/week at current conversion rate.

### Priority 2: Close the "Seat Cushion" Visibility Gap (Week 2-3)

**Why:** "Seat cushion" (41,205 volume) is your highest-volume core category keyword, and the current leader is weak (flagged as vulnerable). You hold only 2.45% click share. This is the single biggest growth lever in your keyword portfolio.

**What to do:**

1. **Launch a Sponsored Brands campaign** targeting "seat cushion," "chair cushions" (28,236 vol), and "office chair cushion" (25,312 vol) as a keyword group
   - Use a Sponsored Brands video ad showcasing the product in use at a desk
   - Bid aggressively — the leader is weak and can be displaced

2. **Optimize your most relevant ASIN** for "seat cushion"
   - Ensure "seat cushion" is in the title, bullet points, and backend keywords
   - Add A+ content with comparison charts showing your cushion vs. alternatives
   - If you have multiple cushion ASINs, ensure they're targeting different long-tail variants rather than cannibalizing each other

3. **Layer in Sponsored Display** retargeting on competitor ASINs ranking #1-3 for "seat cushion"

4. **Also target these related weak-leader terms with the same campaign:**
   - car seat cushion (17,226 vol, 1.09% share)
   - sciatica pain relief devices (17,427 vol, 0.11% share)
   - chair cushions (28,236 vol, 1.02% share)

**Expected impact:** Moving from 2.45% to 5% click share on "seat cushion" alone = ~950 additional clicks/week. Across this keyword cluster (~130K combined volume), a 2-3% share gain = ~3,000-4,000 additional clicks/week.

### Priority 3: Protect Your Strongholds (Ongoing)

**Why:** You hold dominant positions on several keywords. These are revenue anchors — if a competitor takes them, it's hard to win them back.

**What to do:**

1. **"ring sizers for loose rings"** (47,305 vol, 13.27% share) — **High alert.** You're flagged as a weak leader. Competitors see this. Run defensive Sponsored Brands + Sponsored Display on this term. Ensure your listing is the best in class.

2. **"donut pillow"** (15,051 vol, 14.10% share) — Dominant position. Maintain Sponsored Products on this term. Don't reduce budget here.

3. **"knee pillow"** (21,650 vol, 8.63% share) — Healthy but you're not the leader. Consider whether investing to move to #1 is worth the ad spend at this volume.

4. **"coccyx cushion"** (1,770 vol, 15.90% click share) — Your best-performing keyword. Impression share is growing (+0.46 over 12W). This is working — don't change the strategy, just monitor.

**Defensive playbook:** Set up Sponsored Brands headlines on all 4 of these keywords. Create a Brand Store page dedicated to each product category. This makes it harder for competitors to steal your clicks even if they outbid you on Sponsored Products.
